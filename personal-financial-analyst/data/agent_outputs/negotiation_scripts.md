### 1. Internet - Comcast - Current: $89.99/month

#### Market Research
**Competitor Prices (March 2026):**
- Verizon Fios: $59.99-$79.99/month (gigabit speeds)
- Charter Spectrum: $49.99-$69.99/month (comparable speeds)
- AT&T Fiber: $55.00-$74.99/month (where available)
- T-Mobile Home Internet: $50.00/month (fixed wireless)

**Leverage Points:**
- Comcast has high churn rates (customers frequently switch)
- Customer loyalty/tenure discounts available upon request
- Promotional rates for new customers (typically 30-50% off first 12 months)
- Bundle discounts available with TV/phone services
- "Win-back" offers if you mention switching

#### Negotiation Strategy

**Timing:** Call during off-peak hours (Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM - 2 PM) when representatives have more flexibility and time.

**Step 1: Research Before Calling**
- Get a quote from T-Mobile Home Internet at tmobile.com (takes 5 minutes)
- Screenshot or note the exact pricing in your area
- Check if Verizon Fios or AT&T Fiber serve your address

**Step 2: Call Comcast Retention Department**
- Don't call general customer service - ask specifically for "Customer Retention" or "Loyalty Department"
- Phone: 1-800-XFINITY (1-800-934-6489)
- Select "Cancel Service" option in the phone tree (this routes you to retention)

#### Word-for-Word Script

**Opening:**
> "Hi, I've been a Comcast customer for [X] years and I'm calling because I'm concerned about my internet bill. I'm currently paying $89.99/month and I've received offers from [T-Mobile/Verizon/Spectrum] for comparable service at $50-60/month. I'd really like to stay with Comcast but I need to see if you can help me find a better rate."

**If they offer a small discount:**
> "I appreciate that. I want to be transparent - T-Mobile Home Internet is offering me $50/month with no contract and no equipment fees. To stay with Comcast, I'd need to be closer to that price range. Is there anything in the $60-70/month range you can do?"

**If they resist:**
> "I understand your constraints. Can you transfer me to your loyalty or retention department? I've been a customer for [X] years and I'd really prefer to stay, but I need this to make financial sense."

**If they still won't budge:**
> "I'm going to need to think about this. Can you give me a call-back number in case I decide to explore my options? I may be calling back to process a cancellation."

**Escalation Path:**
1. Ask for a supervisor
2. Hang up and call back (different rep, different outcome - try 3x)
3. File a complaint with FCC at fcc.gov/consumers/guides/filing-informal-complaint (this triggers a formal response)
4. Actually cancel and switch to T-Mobile for 30 days, then call back for "win-back" offer

**Expected Outcome:** $15-25/month reduction ($180-300/year)

**Before Calling:**
1. Get quotes from at least 2 competitors online (Geico and Progressive take 10 minutes each)
2. Note your current coverage levels (liability limits, deductible, comprehensive/collision)
3. Check if you qualify for any discounts you're not currently getting

**Calling State Farm:**

- Phone: Listed on your policy card

**Opening:**
> "Hi, I'm calling to review my auto insurance policy. I've been with State Farm for [X] years and I want to make sure I'm getting the best value. I've recently received quotes from other insurers that are significantly lower than what I'm currently paying."

**Sharing Competitor Quotes:**
> "I received a quote from Geico for comparable coverage at $[X]/month. I prefer to stay with State Farm given our relationship, but I'm struggling to justify the $[difference]/month price difference. Can you review my policy to see if there are any discounts I'm not taking advantage of, or if there's a way to lower my premium?"

**Specific Discounts to Ask About:**
